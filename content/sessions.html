The Chronicle Log

The souls bound by destiny to navigate the mists of Barovia.

Session 38: The Abbey and the Seance

Inside, the group met Zelly, an olive-skinned Vistani woman with a prosthetic leg. She revealed that she had returned to the Abbey to check on the Abbott, as she perceived he was becoming increasingly unstable. Zelly also noted that her friend, Van Richten, had returned to the area three months ago.

The group conducted a seance to contact spirits, during which they asked ten questions. The cards identified the spirit as Saint Markovia. When asked if the Abbott had made a deal with Strahd, the "Mist" card appeared, indicating a mysterious and unavoidable situation. The seance also revealed that to restore the Abbott, the party must obtain a specific physical artifact, with signs pointing toward the Amber Temple or the dungeons of Ravenloft as potential locations.

Following the seance, a surge of ethereal wind and a radiant burst of light filled the room, bringing visions of a white-robed woman, a winged man, and a silver statuette, accompanied by intense feelings of humility, hope, defiance, and sorrow. As the visions faded, Cloven, Otto, and Zigfric arrived at the door, expressing concern over having overheard the seance. The party engaged them, and Zigfric—who specifically knows about Sirus—was questioned on how to achieve "perfection". The trio asked us if we could ask Sirus some questions and in return they would proivide us with a secret treasure.

As the party departed the Abbey, they encountered the Abbott, who recognized and reclaimed the necklace Vel had taken from the Golem. The group proceeded toward their wagon to inventory their belongings with Zelly, with plans to travel to the Wolf Den to save Ilya.

Session 38: The Abbey and the Bride of Strahd

After a long rest, Flint distributed gifts to the party: a radiant weapon for Spigot, a mind sharpener for Mel, a sonic disruptor for Vel, and a recoil dampener for Ripley. The party also discovered a sketch of a woman behind a map, noting her resemblance to Dimitri. They learned that Esmerelda is in town staying at the abbey while helping search for the missing Ilya, who escaped on October 22. The party also learned of Zelecka, Dimitri's sister and a werewolf who has not been seen in Krezk for 30 years.

The party traveled to the Abbey of St. Markovia, observing children playing with genuine smiles despite the dark nature of the location. Upon arrival, they encountered stitched-together beings named Otto and Zigfric, assembled from spared parts. The Abbott, who had recently exploded a goat last week, greeted the party and obsessed over "perfection" while staring at a wall. They were brought inside by Cloven Belleview, a hunched patchwork figure with two heads.

The Abbott revealed that the abominations in the courtyard were once regular people. He claimed that Strahd is cursed and that he intends to become the ally Strahd needs. To break the curse on the land, the Abbott is training a being named Vasilka to be Strahd’s bride, utilizing Anna's heart to fill the void in Strahd's own heart. The Abbott requested that the party teach Vasilka about love, a task for which Vel volunteered. During this time, the party learned that a creature named Marzeena had escaped from her room, and that she and Sirus are siblings, with Sirus having been "promoted" to help Strahd. Do not tell the others that Sirus was promoted.

Session 37: Revelations in Krezk

Upon arriving in Krezk, the party encountered a dark-haired woman named Carly, who wielded a rapier and appeared to recognize them. They were also met by Zelly—potentially Esmerelda Davonic—who briefly checked her pockets before departing. The party was approached by the local Baron, Dimitri Krezkov, who seemed to be in charge of the village. During their introduction, they learned that a boy named Ilya was missing, and the Baron’s young daughter, Kala, was also present.

The party gained insight into the local history, including the story of Saint Markovia, who led a rebellion against Castle Ravenloft. They learned of the Abbott, a figure who arrived over a century ago without aging and is rumored to serve Strahd, with strange screams and laughter emanating from his Abbey. While staying at the Baron's cottage, the party assisted with chores, though Vel overheard the Baron attempting to convince his wife, Anna, to flee. Later, the Abbott visited the cottage without an invitation, displaying the ability to transform into an eagle upon his departure.

A tragic truth was revealed: after a werewolf attack left many wounded, Kala had died and Ilya had vanished. The Abbott offered to bring Kala back if Anna provided her heart to create a being named Vasilka, a request that left the parents distressed. Kala had returned, but she was not the same, and the parents had twelve days remaining to make their decision. Flint discovered Kala in her room, where she asked him to escort her to the basement that night to confront the monster that killed her.

In the basement, the party found claw marks, a lupine beast, and damaged silver shackles. They discovered that the Baron’s family line was cursed, with a werewolf bite in their history that manifested when children turned 13. The dark reality was that Ilya had been a werewolf, and after a failed potion, he had grown stronger and killed Kala. The party also investigated magical items, including a wand—a conduit to the fanes—and a green gemstone butterfly that had lost its magic.

Session 36: The Whispering Wall and Arrival in Krezk

Following the collapse of the tower, the party retrieved the staff despite being attacked by druids. Flint secured the staff while Spigot and Ripley focused on the green gem; Ripley cut open the statue to allow Spigot to retrieve it before it detonated from force damage. During the conflict, Ripley rescued Muriel from beneath the debris.

Strahd invited the party to the "Whispering Wall," which he described as the tomb of an ancient god known for offering visions to those who enter. Strahd recounted that he had entered the wall long ago, where he saw a white castle, a city, and heard church bells. Vel entered the wall, where the cold vanished and she found herself beneath the stars, recognizing a melody playing in the air. She encountered the "Ladies Three," who seemed to know her and indicated she was part of their stories. Before leaving through a manifesting archway, Vel was offered a grey and silver gemstone by Strahd; she chose the silver one, which is said to take the bearer to their desired destination. Strahd also revealed a dragon’s eye, a trinket that requires a spark of power to activate.

After returning to the group, the party confirmed that the three gems are tied to the "fanes". They reunited with Davian at the winery, where it was confirmed that the Martikov family are wereravens and spies. Davian provided Spigot with an obsidian whistle, and the party learned of a need to find a token of the Ladies—specifically a "handless spear" buried in the Eastern mountains at the Pillar Stone of Ravenloft.

Following a short rest and a level-up, the party departed for Krezk. Along the road, they encountered a figure in rusty armor and were warned that werewolves have become increasingly bold. Upon arriving at Krezk, the group gained entry by leveraging their wine delivery; once inside, Ripley felt a pull from his scarf, and a mysterious voice called out to the group, suggesting they move closer to share what they were carrying.

The party also noted that Muriel overheard Strahd’s earlier reading, which mentioned "The Dark lord, Master of shadows" and a "Maiden with the raven hair". Additionally, the reading spoke of an innocent on the way to the foden and that the "Wall of whispers await your tribute".

Session 35: Strahd Appears

Muriel provided a map to Yester Hill, where the party observed bright lightning flashes on the boulders and heard distant drumming. Upon their arrival, they found Strahd waiting atop the hill on his nightmare horse; upon seeing Flint, Strahd remarked that the Amber Temple had taken notice of him. Strahd offered the party a reward if they could stop the ritual without his interference, instructing them to meet him at the base of the whispering wall to collect it. Below, thirteen cloaked figures, led by a dark-skinned woman, stood in a circle. Strahd introduced the party to Ludmilla, one of his vampire brides who had been watching the ravens. During her monologue, Ludmilla confirmed that Muriel is a were-raven, noting that Elric had also been a were-raven; when a raven previously captured by Strahd died, it left behind a human corpse. Additionally, Ludmilla stated that Baba Lasaga has sworn to destroy all the Martikovs. Regarding Flint's condition, he is currently unable to summon his cannon or perform magic. He received a message from Seriac, referred to as the "Master of the Hunter," which stated: "Seek out the mountain, find the temple — let's hunt a vampire". After Strahd departed to allow the party to interfere with the ritual, they engaged in battle against Ludmilla and the druids, ultimately stopping the ritual before Ludmilla fled.

Session 34: Keepers of the Feather Revealed

Following pressure from Stefania, Davian revealed that the Martikov family are the Keepers of the Feather, a group he leads. This is a group that opposes Strahd and gather intelligence on him. The winery’s magical production of 3 types of wine was once powered by three enchanted gemstones, all of which have been stolen: one by a woman with a raven (3 months ago), the second by Baba Lasaga’s scarecrows (3 weeks ago), and the third by the Forest Folk (3 days ago). The Martikovs requested assistance at Yester Hill, and on the way, the party met a spirit named Kavan, who revealed that the Druids of Yester Hill have been increasingly active. Kavan explained that before Strahd, there were three tribes, and he had stolen a spear called "Blood Drinker" from the whispering wall to wage war on the others. The party promised to return Svarog’s wand undamaged in exchange for information on a powerful weapon. While the party continued toward Yester Hill, they were intercepted by Strahd. During a tense conversation, Spiggot refused to show the respect Strahd demanded, prompting Strahd to attack and kill Flint. After Strahd departed, the amber shards on Flint's body came to life, forcing Flint to choose between death, his mother’s shard, or Isek’s shard; he chose Isek’s. Flint was revived, but he was physically altered, possessing ashy grey skin, no longer breathing, and sporting an arm turning red with spines. Following his resurrection, Flint pushed onwards with the party and began walking toward Yester Hill barely saying a word.

Session 33: Winery Victory

On the upper floor, the party discovered a massive blighted tree with red thorns and blood-filled sap that had taken root in the courtyard coming up to the upper level. The druid leader's staff, identified as the wand of Loghoth, manifested and the druid spoke of "Winter Splinter," warning that victory would be short-lived and that the nectar of victory would turn to ash upon their lips by dawn. Spiggot destroyed the wand, causing it to shatter into hundreds of pieces, which immediately caused all nearby blights to freeze and crumble. The massive tree subsequently collapsed into a thick, greyish sludge as a cleansing rain began to fall, washing away the blood and dirt from the area. Following these events, the Martikov family emerged from the tunnels below, and the party was awarded 2000 XP.

Session 32: Winery Battle Continues

The party engaged in a continued fight against blights on the main floor of the winery. As the druid leader fell, his final words were, "Winter Splinter is coming". Following this, the group proceeded to investigate the main floor of the winery.

Session 31: Winery Cellar / Forest Folk Lore

Below the winery, the party found Stefania and her children — Martin, Vigal, Claudio, Adrian, Elvir (Elric's twin) and an infant—hiding in a small stone cavern. They learned that druids had invaded the winery three days prior, and that the flying blight creatures they faced originated from the "Gultiest Tree," a cursed tree on Yester Hill created by the witch Baba Lasaga, who resides in the Ruins of Berez. The druid leader is reportedly carrying a magical staff that powers these blights, and the party noted that they must break this staff to stop the creatures. The party discovered a box containing three wolf teeth marked with runic inscriptions, which possess transmutation magic; stabbing oneself with a tooth releases a blast of cold wind that clears paths and turns the tooth into mist. Mel successfully utilized one of these teeth to clear a passage through yellow moss within the tunnel. Additionally, the party learned of internal family conflicts, including missing prized gemstones belonging to Davian and the history of Elric, who had joined a rebellion against Ravenloft three months ago and never returned.

Session 30: The Winery / Meeting the Martikovs

After reading the Sacred Tome of Strahd and earning 750 XP (See Lore and Secrets for details), the party turned their attention to the Wizard of Wines winery, which was two days late on a wine delivery. The winery's owner, Davian Martikov, appeared anxious and short-tempered, requesting the party investigate the situation. Before departing, the party purchased a map of Krezk and a partial map of Argynvostholt. Muriel Martikov, described as short and dusky-skinned with a blue streak in her hair, joined the group as a guide. Although Ireena requested to stay behind, fearing she attracted trouble, the party insisted she accompany them. During their travel to the winery the party encountered a broad-shouldered mountain folk druid who fled upon seeing them. Muriel warned the party to avoid druid lairs, and shortly after, the group defended themselves against attacking vines, where they discovered a three-pointed star engraving—the symbol of the Ladies of the Fanes (3 - Weaver, Seeker, Huntress). Upon arriving at the vineyard, they were attacked by flying scarecrows, leading Davian to guide the party through a hidden trapdoor in the nearby woods, a location Muriel did not recognize.

Session 29: Leo Delinzia / Sacred Tome of Strahd

After the ritual and the confrontation with the Lich Master Leo Delinzia, the party learned of his history as the former head of Strahd’s castle guard. After Strahd murdered Sergei, Leo ordered his guards to fire arrows at Strahd, which pierced his soul but failed to kill him. In revenge, Strahd hunted Leo, who eventually became a Lich hiding at Saint Markovia's on Mount Baratok, where he had sealed Lavina Wachner—an ancestor of Fiona—in a stone vault to starve. Once his enchanted ruby was destroyed, Leo was finally able to die; Flint delivered the final blow (again) and claimed the Deep Crimson Red Tome, which is the Sacred Tome of Strahd (Card 1 Prophecy). Additional developments from the aftermath include: Erasmus shared that Strahd is often seen traveling through the plane on a nightmare horse. Viktor provided the party with a Spirit Mirror and a Lantern of Revealing. It was confirmed that Stella is back in her body. The party earned 1750 XP and planned to assist Erwin with the wine shortage in the morning.

Session 28: Town Square Trial / Ethereal Plane Ritual

Rahadin presented Milovogh and Henrik to Lady Fiona to administer justice on Strahd's behalf. The party intervened and negotiated the sentences, resulting in 15 lashings for Henrik and the loss of a hand for Milovogh. Later that night, the party convened in Lady Fiona's cellar, which featured walls marked by daggers and a stone floor carved with a pentagram. Viktor performed a ritual that allowed the group to enter the Ethereal Plane, where they found Stella struggling to maintain her tangibility. They were also joined by the spirit of Erasmus Van Richten, a cheerful 14-year-old boy. Stella entered the binding circle for a 10-minute window and delivered a cryptic message: "Come for the blood that is owed. Deep Crimson Red Tome.".;

Session 27: Nightmare Hags / Strahd's Invitation

The battle at the mill concluded when the party defeated Morgantha’s daughters, who had transformed into Nightmare Hags, while Flint finished off Morgantha with a critical hit from an upcast Guiding Bolt to retrieve her Heartstone. On the walk back, the party encountered one of the guards who had previously attacked Velastra and engaged him in conversation. Upon returning to the inn, they found Rahadin waiting for them, who delivered an invitation from Strahd to Castle Ravenloft in six days, promising safe passage both ways. Additionally, Mel successfully used Remove Curse to heal Spiggot of his lycanthropy, and the Heartstone was confirmed as regained for use with the layline in the Wachter house cellar for the rescue ritual. The session ended with the news that Milivoj was set to be executed in the town square.

Session 26: Old Bonegrinder / Morganta Defeated

Upon encountering the party, Morgantha immediately recognized Velastra, addressing her by the name "Echo". During the confrontation, Ripley went upstairs with Bella, one of Morgantha's daughters. When Morgantha attempted to cast a spell, Viktor successfully countered it. Ripley subsequently forced open a door on the third level to discover Franz's children, Josephia and Camel, hidden inside.

Session 25: Franz's Children / Stella Ritual Planned

Franz’s sister reported that Morgantha had taken his children, Fiodor and Mertyl, following a desperate bargain Franz made to see his deceased wife in his dreams. The witch had also taken the children of their neighbors, Josephia and Camel. To rescue the children, Viktor devised a plan for a binding ritual to be performed in Fiona's cellar, which is situated on a leyline and contains a circle capable of preventing hags from escaping into the Ethereal Plane. Lady Fiona agreed to assist the party and brought along four acolytes named Andres, Aleksandra, Boris, and Meroona to complete the ritual. During their journey to the Old Bonegrinder, a druid intercepted the group and engaged in a conversation with Fiona, who was addressed as the "Daughter of Mother Night". The party subsequently had to battle their way through a series of spiders and twig blights to reach their destination. Upon arriving at the Old Bonegrinder mill, Vel discovered a bag, a charm bracelet, and a paper containing the Hags true names.

Session 24: Stella's Soul / Baron Abdicates

Following the death of Izek, the party recovered a silvered battle axe, 35 gp, and an amber shard that shares the same Abjuration properties as Flint's. Mel performed a ceremony, and the group delivered Izek's head to Lady Fiona Wachter. At 5:00 a.m., writing appeared on their window asking them to "Help Victor" and "Please — Erasmus". Viktor confirmed that the spirit in the mirror is Stella Wachter, whose soul became trapped in the Ethereal Plane three months ago following a failed teleportation circle experiment, where she has since been pursued by a Gallowspeaker. To perform a rescue ritual, the party now needs to obtain a Heartstone. Additional developments in Vallaki included: Franz at the refugee camp is wasting away, having traded his children to Morganta. The Baron publicly abdicated his position. Lady Fiona Wachter took the stage and claimed leadership of Vallaki.

Session 23: Festival of the Blazing Sun / Isek Killed

During preparations for the festival, the party successfully infiltrated the Baron’s kitchen where Velastra stole a bottle of wine to serve as a decoy. Velastra, Flint, and Spiggot spent the day tracking Izek through his afternoon rounds at the gates and his visits to three different houses. While Izek visited Blinsky to inquire about an unfinished Ireena doll, Velastra successfully swapped his wine bottle for the poisoned decoy. Following this, the party trailed Izek to Lake Zarovich, where he consumed the poisoned wine. Once the poison took effect, the group attacked and killed him.

Session 22: Aragail / Arabelle's Prophecy

The journey back to Valaki was marked by significant encounters, as the party was blocked by Zolt and Matilda, trappers from Krezk. During these events, Izek was found to be Ireena’s biological brother, while a man named Aragail confronted the party about Arabelle's disappearance before ultimately backing down. Furthermore, Van Richten shed his Rictavio persona and revealed that Arabelle was safe within his wagon, noting that she had been kidnapped by a vampire named Esher. While talking to the group, Arabelle experienced a premonition, speaking in an older voice to deliver a grim prophecy stating "In darkest night, the light shall flee, No dawn to break, with no reprieve. From the grave, the dead shall climb, Their restless march a baleful sign. At castle’s peak, a heart beats red, Its hunger deep and ever-fed. Should silence fall, the skies shall crack, A thousand souls in torment black." and a coming torment of souls. Following these revelations, Earnst confirmed the party's plot to assassinate Izek during a meeting in an alley. He told the group how Isek's day normally goes: He receives a case with 2 wine bottles in the morning from Theresa then does his rounds. He the goes to a site off the grounds of Valaki for a time. He is always done the first bottle before this time. The butler provided a poisoned bottle of Red Dragon Crush, to switch out. With the plan set in motion, the party visited Luvash and acquired treasure, including studded leather and various items valued at 250 GP.

Session 21: Kazan's Tower Explored

Upon arriving at Kazan's Tower, the party encountered a wagon parked at the entrance with a sign reading "Keep out — Home of Esmeralda Davenir". The wagon radiated conjuration magic, while the tower's iron door, inscribed with Kazan's name, was protected by abjuration magic. Following an attack by gargoyles, an investigation of the tower yielded burnt parchment, specifically Van Richten's journal. An upper level of the tower contained a rotted crib, and the journal revealed that Van Richten's child had been murdered by a vampire. Additionally, the group broke into the wagon and discovered several items, including spell scrolls of Remove Curse and Mirror Image, a Tarokka card ("The Hooded One"), and a beaded bracelet wrapped around a silver dagger. Additional items found in the wagon included a steel mirror, wooden stakes, a spyglass, a 50-foot coil of hempen rope, and three vials of perfume.

Session 20: Vel's Abduction

The night began with a harrowing abduction when Izek Strazni and his guards used a blade to Flint’s throat to force Velastra’s surrender. They transported her outside the town walls to interrogate her, where she was ultimately pressured into revealing that Ireena was hiding at the church. The following morning, Vel showed up and and after being healed by Mel she warned the party that Izek was actively hunting Ireena. The party immediately rushed to the church, confirmed Ireena’s safety, and welcomed her as a full member of their group again. The party later traveled to Lake Zarovich to investigate the wolf shack linked to Arabelle’s disappearance, though they found no signs of a struggle in the presence of the werewolf brothers, Szoldar and Yevgeni. During their travels, the group discovered a piece of white fabric in the river reeds embroidered with "RVR," which they suspect is connected to Rudolph Van Richten, and observed animated scarecrows and saplings following them along the tree line. The session concluded as the group arrived at the decrepit Kazan’s Tower, noting a barrel-topped wagon parked at the entrance.

Session 19: Dinner with Darkness

The party's situation in Vallaki turned critical when they discovered wanted posters for Ireena, prompting them to rush to the church to ensure she remained safely in hiding. During a formal dinner at the Wachter Estate, Lady Fiona Wachter proposed a treacherous deal: she offered to seize control of Vallaki and welcome the refugees if the party assassinated Izek, whom she identified as having a weakness for wine. While returning from the dinner, the party defiantly released Udo’s mother from the stocks, but upon reaching the Blue Water Inn, they discovered Izek had destroyed the interior in his search for Ireena. The situation culminated in a harrowing confrontation that night when Izek broke into the room where Flint and Velastra were sleeping. Threatening Flint’s sleeping life by holding a blade to his throat, Izek forced Velastra to surrender to him. The session concluded with Velastra being taken captive by Izek, leaving the party in a precarious and dangerous position.

Session 18: Secrets of the Burgomaster's Mansion

The party spent hours researching in the Burgomaster's library, confirming that the original Ireena Strazni—sister of Izek—passed away 18 years ago at the age of four; however, it remains unknown if the Ireena currently traveling with the party is the same child. During their investigation, they encountered the Baron’s son, Viktor Vallakovich, who admitted to unintentionally trapping the soul of a friend—identified as Stella Wachter—in the Ethereal Plane while attempting to construct a teleportation circle. Viktor also identified the ring found at the crime scene as bearing the symbol of the legendary wizard Kazan, whose tower is located on Lake Baratok. Additionally, Viktor provided Flint with a book titled Ethereal Entities. Beyond the library, the party’s search of the mansion uncovered further disturbing truths: Ripley discovered Udo the shoemaker being held captive in a locked closet. The party explored Izek's room, which was filled with lifelike dolls made in Ireena's likeness, confirming the extent of his obsession. It was noted that Slodor, a wolf hunter, possesses a shack near the location where Arabelle vanished.

Session 17: Threads Pull Tight

The party began their day attempting to intercept Morgantha before she entered Vallaki, but they were unsuccessful; her presence deeply unsettled Ripley. Upon traveling to the Vistani camp for Arabelle’s birthday, they learned the girl had been kidnapped, leaving her cousin Alexei devastated. At the Luna River crime scene, the party discovered the decapitated body of a man named Yan. A dusk-skinned elf, named Kasimir (who came to this camp over Mt. Ghakis in the past week) retrieved the ring bearing symbols of evocation and necromancy. Investigation of the crime scene revealed three sets of tracks: one leading to a tree trunk, one toward the river's edge, and one belonging to the deceased man, Yan. To further research the ring, the party visited the library at the Burgomaster's mansion, where they spoke with the Burgomaster about the upcoming festival and the wood being used as an offer to the morning lord. In exchange for information on the ring, the party agreed to investigate a spirit haunting the mansion’s mirrors—a phenomenon that has been occurring for the past two months—and was advised to consult the cook, Tarreska.

Session 16: Blood in the Coffin Maker's House

The fight against the vampire spawn concluded with the fleeing of Ludmilla and Anastrasya, while Volenta escaped by transforming into a bat and slipping through a crack in the wall. Afterward, the party returned the stolen holy bones to Father Lucian, successfully restoring the divine protection of the Church of Saint Andral. That evening, the group received a formal invitation to dine at the Wachter Estate. During private meditation, Flint communicated with the entity residing in his Amber Shard, who identified itself as Norganas, the Finger of Oblivion. Norganas offered Flint additional power—specifically the ability to cast Bestow Curse at the 5th level once per day and the gain of temporary hit points equal to his proficiency bonus whenever he sees a creature reach zero hit points—in exchange for traveling to the Amber Temple. This pact comes at a cost, however: Flint’s blood has turned black, and he now has one fewer hit die available to him. Meanwhile, Velastra pressed Rictavio for information, leading him to reveal that he is a secret agent for the Keepers of the Feather, a group that opposes Strahd, though he still refused to disclose his real name. While Vel was throwing herself at Rictavio, he noted that his heart belongs to someone else and that his tell is when its eyes flip upwards.

Session 15: The Coffin Maker's Secret

The investigation into the missing Bones of Saint Andral led the party to Milivoj, who, under the magical scrutiny of Detect Thoughts and Zone of Truth, confessed that he had stolen the relics and sold them to the local coffin maker, Henrik van der Voort, to support his struggling family. Milivoj also noted that he had been influenced by a suspicious man who had been lurking around. Upon arriving at the coffin maker's shop, the party located the stolen bones inside a wardrobe. Immediately after, a Blinsky toy triggered an alarm, and Vel was struck by a poisoned dart. As the party descended into the shop, they were confronted by three vampire spawn—Ludmilla, Anastrasya, and Volenta—who referred to the group as "skinny new plaything".

Session 14: Shadows of Vallaki

The party’s morning in Vallaki began with news of the upcoming Festival of the Blazing Sun, marked by the Baron’s insistence that "All will be well!". During a parade led by Baron Vallakovich and his enforcer, Izek, a woman crying out for her missing son was seized by guards and dragged to the stocks. Throughout the procession, Izek kept his cold, unsettling gaze fixed specifically on Ireena. Seeking more information on Izek, the party visited Blinsky’s Toy Shop, where they discovered a doll made in Ireena’s likeness that Izek had obsessively commissioned. Vel successfully impersonated Izek using a disguise self spell to retrieve the latest doll from the toymaker. Later, the group visited the Wachter Estate, but the residence remained dark and unresponsive to their attempts to gain entry. They shifted their focus to the Church of St. Andral, where Father Lucian revealed that the holy bones of Saint Andral, which provide protection to the town, had been stolen. While investigating the area where the bones were kept, Flint discovered several critical clues left behind, including a piece of wool fabric, some rust-colored dirt on the floorboards, and blood splattered on the shattered glass of a broken window.

Session 13: Tales & Whispers in Vallaki

The party arrived at The Blue Water Inn, Vallaki’s sole tavern, after a long journey, feeling watched by a mysterious figure lurking in the shadows outside. Inside, they were welcomed by innkeepers Erwin and Danika Martikov. The atmosphere was charged with local tension; while the inn served wolf steak and beet stew, the environment felt oppressive. Notable patrons included two haunted wolf hunters, Szoldar and Yevgeni, as well as the wealthy brothers Nikolai and Karl Wachter, who openly expressed their disdain for Baron Vallakovich's rule. The room grew lively as Rictavio, an eccentric carnival ringmaster, entertained the patrons with dark, humorous stories, including the tale of "Count Belasco and the Widow Olivenca" (See Lore for Story). However, the mood shattered when a drunk patron mentioned "the Stupid Baron’s Song," causing an immediate, terrified silence to fall over the tavern. Amidst the unease, Ripley snuck upstairs to consume a Dream Pie, slipping into a deep, vivid trance. Meanwhile, Flint identified the silver dragon amulet as a relic of the long-forgotten Order of the Silver Dragon, noting that the item appeared dormant but bound to a greater command.

Session 12: Journey to Vallaki

During the trek to Vallaki, the party encountered stone statues of a raven and a 3-pointed star, while Mel observed bats flying toward Castle Ravenloft. They stopped at an old, decrepit windmill where they met Morganta, a woman selling "Dream Pies" who claimed her daughters, Bella and Ophalia, lived there with her. Ripley consumed one of the pies and fell into a trance, from which he was only recovered after being slapped by Spiggot. Upon reaching Lake Zarovich, Ireena experienced a sense of recognition, unsure if it stemmed from a dream or a genuine memory. The journey grew perilous when the party was ambushed by five wolves, two of which were werewolves. During the combat, Velastra and Spiggot were bitten, resulting in wounds that refused to heal. A mysterious voice offered Flint power, which he accepted in exchange for the Bestow Curse spell (5th level). A raven-human named Blue aided the party in the fight but departed abruptly after noticing bats. Upon arriving at the Vallaki refugee camp, the party encountered hostile refugees who recognized Ireena and blamed her for the attacks in the village of Barovia, suggesting she should have been surrendered to Strahd. Ireena chose to stay behind to explain the situation to the refugees, who were living in makeshift tents; many were wounded, and some were addicted to the Dream Pies. The party met Isaac, a man with a twisted arm, and a young woman named Magda. Before entering the town, the guards, armed with silver spears, confiscated all of the party's silver while repeating the town's motto: "All will be well".

Session 11: The Knight on the Bridge

Following Madame Eva's reading, the party traveled westward, soon to be accompanied by a Vistana named Eliza who joined their ranks. Along the road, they encountered a skeleton mounted on a horse and holding an unlit lantern, which was traveling toward the village of Barovia. During the journey, Velastra discovered a broken silver pendant shaped like a dragon; Flint inspected it, found no magic, and returned it. As they continued, the party was stalked by wolves that exhibited an unnatural level of intelligence. At one point, Flint was downed during an attack, but a dark power offered him a hand; Flint accepted the aid and was restored to full health and was provided power at a cost. Eventually, the party reached a bridge where they encountered a spectral knight, a member of the Order of the Silver Dragon who had forgotten his name in life. The knight, who remains oathbound to this location due to having raised his voice against his commander, asked the party if they opposed the lord of Barovia. He directed them to travel "beyond the ruined town, then south upon the ash-grey path" to reach Argynvostholt, where they could find Ser Godfrey Gwilym for assistance with weapons against Strahd. The knight concluded the encounter with a dire warning: "Beware those who walk the skins of wolves to the west. Their hunger for human flesh cannot be sated".

Session 10: Madame Eva's Reading

Before leaving the Tser Pool encampment for their reading, the party engaged Arturi (an old aquantance of Rudolph Van Richten) in a "Game of Stories," where Mel's victory earned them a brass spyglass—which he gifted to Flint — a scholarly manuscript titled A Study of the Werewolf’s Curse — A painted wooden hairclip, from Ireena — A worn deck of tarokka cards , from Stanomir. Upon leaving the Vistani camp, they sought out the mysterious Madame Eva. She performed a chilling Tarokka reading, revealing that their history is tied to a betrayed noble soul who guards secrets of Strahd. See "Tarokka Card Reading" for full details. The session ended in a terrifying display of supernatural power; as the final card was revealed, the surrounding candles were extinguished, and Madame Eva was seized by a possession-like tremor. The true weight of their situation was made clear. Finally, Madam Eva requested that we purchase a Blinksies Toy for her Niece southwest of Valaki for her 10th birthday in 2 days.

Session 9: Encounter with Strahd

The party’s journey toward the Tser Pool was interrupted by a harrowing encounter with Strahd von Zarovich, who emerged from his carriage — a pale, towering figure with a ruby at his throat. He handed a mysterious trinket to Flint which kept him busy and seized Velastra's mirror. He mocked their struggle, treating them as mere playthings in his grand design, and insisted that the Tarokka cards were instruments of deception. After unsettling the group with his chilling presence, Strahd granted them their lives for the moment, but not before handing a mysterious trinket to Flint and seizing Velastra's mirror. In the aftermath, Velastra fell into an eerie trance, hearing Strahd recite a verse that seemed to be a deliberate, dark counter-prophecy to their earlier Tarokka reading. Upon finally reaching the Vistani camp at Tser Pool, the group sought a moment of respite and guidance, where they were approached by Stanomir and each bestowed with ominous, prophetic titles that reflected their growing involvement in the land’s curse: Velastra became "The Star That Fell Twice," Spiggot "Twice Born Exile," Ripley "Masked Fang," Flint "Echo of Gears," and Mel "The Quiet Watcher." These titles solidified their roles in the deepening mystery of Barovia, leaving them to wonder if these labels were signs of a destiny they could master, or merely new chains forged by the vampire lord. Session 8: Strained Hope in Barovia

As the village of Barovia reeled from its recent tragedies, the party found a flicker of hope after returning the holy symbol to Father Donavich, whose faith—and divine powers—were instantly restored. The group also uncovered a hidden supply cache buried by the legendary vampire hunter Van Richten beneath an ancient oak tree, which contained a personal memento for Ireena: a tarnished moonstone and lapis lazuli bracelet. The accompanying note from her parentsread "Tatianna: May you find your path, and may you find light even in the darkest places. - Mother & Father". This offered a glimmer of light in the gloom, and with her resolve solidified, Ireena officially joined the party. Amidst the lingering rumors of Van Richten’s continued survival in disguise and reports of a local woman named Mary frantically searching for her daughter, Gertruda, who had fled to Vallaki with her horse Nori, the party prepared for the journey west. However, their departure was immediately challenged by a sinister omen. As they set out to escort Ireena toward safety, they were confronted on the road by the unmistakable, ominous silhouette of Strahd’s carriage, steered by the transformed vampire spawn, Esher.

Session 7: The Funeral

The party attended the somber funeral of Kolyan Indirovich, led by a broken Father Donavich. The priest confessed that he had surrendered his holy symbol to his son, Doru, to aid a failed rebellion three months prior—a betrayal of his duties that left him devoid of faith. He revealed that this insurrection, inspired by the writings of Van Richten and guided by a foreign scholar named Aranic, resulted in horrific consequences: Doru was transformed into a vampire spawn currently trapped in the church’s undercroft, and his fellow combatant, Esher, was similarly cursed. The tragedy weighed heavily on the group as they navigated the grief of the survivors, including the realization that Gertrude, Doru’s girlfriend, had long since fled to the safety of Vallaki. Caught between the mourning of the living and the horrors of the undead, the party resolved to take action. Recognizing that their only hope for survival and the protection of the marked Ireena lay in escaping the clutches of the village, they committed to escorting her west toward the relative safety of Vallaki.

Session 6: The Battle at the Walls

The party was thrust into a desperate defense of Barovia when a raven guided them to confront an incoming undead assault. They fought alongside the village locals, including the determined shopkeeper Biltrath, during which the true extent of Strahd’s reach was confirmed as Ireena suffered yet another bite. In the wake of the battle, the group faced the tragic loss of the burgomaster, Kolyan, whose death was clearly the work of the vampire lord. The aftermath of the attack brought a far more terrifying presence: Rahadin, Strahd’s dusk elf chamberlain, who arrived atop a phantom steed heralded by the ghastly, fading screams of a thousand souls. During this encounter, Mel’s knife reacted unnervingly to the chamberlain’s proximity. Rahadin stood unmoved by the chaos, delivering a chilling warning from his master before departing. With the burgomaster dead and a dark ultimatum now looming over them, the party finds themselves deeper in the crosshairs of Strahd’s cold, calculated designs.

Session 5: Blood of the Vine Tavern

At the Blood of the Vine Tavern, the party met Ismark Kolyanovich, son of the local burgomaster Kolyan, who revealed that his sister Ireena has been targeted by Strahd. Tensions in the village are high, with a mob led by Alenka actively trying to surrender Ireena to the dark lord to appease him. The party also learned of the local tragedies, specifically the plight of the priest’s son, Doru, who was turned into a vampire spawn after a failed rebellion three months ago. Hints of a deeper destiny surfaced as they discovered that Ireena, as a child, had found the mysterious Pillar of Ragaloth. The group was introduced to several village residents, including the salty but brave mercantile owner Biltrath Cantemere, his nephew Perry Wimple, and the tragic loss of Biltrath's sister Martha and her husband, Dragomere, to the undead.

Session 4: First Steps into Barovia

The final confrontation within the Durst Mansion reached a violent conclusion as Flint struck down Walter, finally ending the abomination’s torment. As the mansion collapsed around them, the party managed a narrow, desperate escape, only to realize the moment they reached the open air that they were no longer in Daggerford. Disoriented and surrounded by a thick, clinging mist that warped their path, they found that all roads led only westward. The trail ahead bore grim signs of a struggle: a corpse marred by savage claw marks, ominous paw prints, and a discarded, tarnished copper compass which Flint confirmed held no magical properties. After forty minutes of trekking through the unnatural gloom, the party arrived at a set of towering iron gates inscribed with the foreboding message: 'The Horseman Rides. The Seer Spoke True.' They pressed onward for another two miles, the atmosphere growing increasingly oppressive until the first structures of the village of Barovia materialized from the fog. As they entered the village proper, the party realized with heavy hearts that they had been transported to the year 735, trapped within the borders of a dark and unfamiliar domain.

Session 3: The Fall of the Dursts

The final confrontation within the Durst House reached a violent conclusion as Flint struck down Walter, finally bringing an end to the abomination’s tormented existence. The victory was immediate but short-lived; as the source of the house's corruption was severed, the structure began a rapid, violent collapse around the party. They managed a narrow escape, scrambling out of the wreckage just as the walls gave way. However, the safety they expected to find outside was a cruel illusion. As they stepped away from the ruins, they were instantly enveloped by the thick, encroaching Mists of Barovia. Unbeknownst to the party, their escape from the house had only served to deliver them directly into the heart of Barovia, Strahd’s domain.

Session 2: The Durst Mansion

The party’s investigation into the Durst House quickly descended into a nightmare as they navigated the mansion’s labyrinthine secrets, uncovering hidden chambers and defiled shrines that exposed the full, grotesque extent of the family’s corruption. Walter's crib had the inscription of "He is the Ancient. He is the Land" (A reference to Strahd). Amidst these grim discoveries, the party secured an amber amulet key, a pivotal artifact that seems inextricably linked to the house’s dark power. The tension peaked when the group came face-to-face with the pulsating, abominable form of Walter.

Session 1: Daggerford & the Orphanage

The party’s shared roots in the Daggerford orphanage were shaken when Grigor One-Eye revealed that a mysterious, cloaked stranger had been inquiring about them, brandishing unsettlingly specific knowledge of their past. He mentioned Zelly, a companion they had grown up alongside, was potentially the true child of their foster mother. This shocking discovery fundamentally alters the group's understanding of their upbringing and leaves them questioning the true nature of the bonds they forged in the orphanage. This prompted a return to the site of their youth, where they discovered that their childhood was inextricably linked to the dark history of the Durst family. Among the haunting remnants of that time—including children clutching stuffed toy wolves—they uncovered ties to Gustav, Elizabeth, and the siblings Thorn and Rose, while also learning that their former nanny, Klara (“Lady Watcher”), may have been the mother of the ill-fated Walter.
