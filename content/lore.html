Learned Lore & Revelations

Forbidden knowledge unpeeled from ancient scripts, vampire diaries, and dark covenants.

Strahd's History — From His Own Tome

Written in his own hand: 'I am the Ancient. I am the Land.' He was once a great warrior-king, just and powerful, but years of war hollowed him out. All goodness slipped from his life. His army settled in the Valley of Barovia and took power with none of the grace they claimed. He called his family — long out of power — to settle with him at Castle Ravenloft. They arrived with his younger brother Sergei, who was handsome and young, everything Strahd no longer was. He hated Sergei for both. A young woman named Tatyana fell in love with Sergei and called Strahd only 'Old One' and 'brother.' They were betrothed. On the wedding day, Strahd made a pact with Death, killed Sergei, and sealed it with his blood. Tatyana fled in despair and threw herself from the castle walls — a thousand feet into the mists. No trace was ever found. Castle guards fired arrows at Strahd. They pierced him to his soul but didn't kill him. He became undead forever. 'Vampyr' is his name now. He hunts Tatyana to this day: 'She cannot run forever — and I have nothing but time.'

Strahd's Powers and Weaknesses — From His Own Tome

From the Tome: the sun is against him, but little else can harm him. A stake through his heart only holds him immobile — it does not kill him. But one weapon terrifies him: 'That cursed sword that Sergei brought! I must dispose of that awful tool! I fear and hate it as much as the sun.' The Sunsword, brought by Sergei as a wedding gift, is the one thing Strahd genuinely fears. He is trying to get rid of it. He sleeps far below Ravenloft, among the dead. His conquered wealth is buried beneath the belfry, 'sealed behind a reminder of the treasure I once lost' — Tatyana. He says: 'Yet one day, I shall rise again from the ashes of my glory and reclaim what is rightfully mine.'

Strahd's Counter-Reading — Vel's Trance

After Strahd left, Vel fell into a trance and eavesdropped his voice speaking in verse: 'She thinks the cards speak truth, that rivers can be dammed / The Sword lies in the hand of the Dead / The Ally walks in the Shadow / Cast a light on the Traitor and call him friend / They will trust the wrong soul, let them chase a ghost.' This appears to be Strahd directly mocking or subverting the Tarokka reading. What it may mean: 'The Sword lies in the hand of the Dead' — the Sunsword may be with a dead person or guarded by undead. 'The Ally walks in the Shadow' — the Vistana ally (Tarokka Card 4) may be hiding, untrustworthy, or in danger. 'Cast a light on the Traitor and call him friend' — someone the party already trusts will betray them. 'They will trust the wrong soul, let them chase a ghost' — the party will be misled. Whether Strahd was telling the truth, lying to destabilise them, or both is unknown. Every major ally deserves caution.

Tatyana and Ireena — The Eternal Hunt

Strahd's Tome: 'I have often hunted for Tatyana. I have even felt her within my grasp, though she fled from me once again. But she cannot run forever.' Strong evidence throughout the campaign suggests Ireena is the latest reincarnation of Tatyana — reborn across centuries in Barovia, drawn back again and again. Ireena has the same red hair. She appeared in the Slavich Woods as a child with no explanation. She recognises Lake Zarovich from a dream. Strahd claims her as his. His minions never directly attack her. If Ireena is Tatyana reborn, keeping her safe is about far more than protecting one person.

The Ladies of the Fanes — The Three-Pointed Star

Three ancient goddesses worshipped in Barovia before Strahd's arrival. Their symbol is a three-pointed star. Three aspects: the Weaver (magic and creation), the Seeker (ravens and knowledge), the Huntress (wolves and the wild). Strahd claimed all three Fanes and bent their servants to his will. The Keepers of the Feather (raven network) may be descended from servants of the Seeker. The werewolf connection near Lake Baratok may relate to the Huntress. The symbol has appeared: carved in stone on the road to Vallaki (Session 12) and near Kazan's Tower.

Norganas / Seriac — The Amber Shard Warning

The entity in Flint's shard goes by Norganas (Mother's shard; Finger of Oblivion) and Seriac (Isek's shard; Master of the Hunter). All Amber Shards — including Isek's — share the same Abjuration properties and originate from the same source: the Amber Temple. The shards cannot be damaged or crossed by magical or divine energy; magic passes through them only over years or decades. Powers granted to Flint: Updated Character sheet, can cast fire bolt and scorching ray (with a cost) at will. Costs: black blood, left arm turning red with barbed spires. Latest offer: Desperate Hunger — wants Flint to become a predator. Final message: 'Seek out the mountain, find the temple — let's hunt a vampire.' This entity is dangerous and self-serving. Treat with extreme caution.

Keepers of the Feather — Full Roster

A secret network opposing Strahd. All members are were-ravens or closely connected. Confirmed members: Davian Martikov (leader), Erwin Martikov, Danika Martikov, Stefania Martikov, Muriel (were-raven), Elric Martikov (were-raven — deceased). Van Richter is also a member or close ally. Their economic base is the Wizards of Wine Winery. Their symbol connects to the three-pointed star (Seeker aspect of the Ladies of the Fanes). Blue may also be connected — Van Richter claims not to know who he is. Baba Lasaga has declared war on them. Strahd warned in Session 35 that ravens cannot be trusted — but he would say exactly that.

Arabelle's Prophecy

When the party saved Arabelle, her eyes rolled back and an older voice spoke through her: 'In the darkest night, the light shall flee, No dawn to break, with no reprieve. "In darkest night, the light shall flee, No dawn to break, with no reprieve. From the grave, the dead shall climb, Their restless march a baleful sign. At castle’s peak, a heart beats red, Its hunger deep and ever-fed. Should silence fall, the skies shall crack, A thousand souls in torment black." The tone resembled Madame Eva. Arabelle has premonitions and was deliberately targeted by Strahd — she is significant to his plans or poses a threat to them.

Rictavio's Story

Listen to the Full Story:

The tale of "Count Belasco and the Widow Olivenca," as told by Rictavio, tells the story of a vain vampire count who resided in the cellar of an old manor managed by his servant, Igren. To hide the fact that he was balding, the Count commanded Igren to purchase a beautiful raven-black wig to maintain his youthful appearance.

The Count had his sights set on one of his guests, an elderly, blind widow named Olivenca, whom he intended to feed upon. However, due to her poor vision, Olivenca remained oblivious to his predatory nature. One night, the Count went out to hunt but struggled to find a victim and eventually found himself caught by the arrival of morning.

In a panic, the Count fled back to the inn, where he encountered Olivenca. When he revealed his intention to suck her blood, Olivenca—who was carrying a collection of her late husband's rare rocks and minerals—violently struck the Count in the head with her purse. The impact knocked the Count to the floor and sent his wig tumbling off his head. Exposed by his vanity and unable to hide his hairless scalp, the Count was quickly incinerated by the rising sun, leaving behind nothing but a "smoldering heap of ash and a third smoking wig". Rictavio ended the story with a pun, remarking that it was quite a "hairy rising experience," much to the amusement of the tavern patrons.

The Krezk Curse and the Abbott's Experiments

The village of Krezk suffers from a dark, hereditary curse tied to a werewolf bite in the Krezkov bloodline, manifesting after a child turns 13. This lineage has resulted in tragedy, including the death of the Baron’s daughter, Kala, at the hands of her werewolf brother, Ilya. Within the nearby Abbey of St. Markovia, the ageless entity known as the Abbott has been conducting disturbing experiments, transforming regular people into "abominations" like Cloven Belleview, Otto, and Zigfric. The Abbott claims to be preparing for a greater purpose: he is crafting a bride for Strahd named Vasilka, utilizing the heart of the Baron's wife, Anna, to fill the void in Strahd's own heart and purportedly break the curse upon the land. The party has been enlisted to assist in "teaching" this construct the nature of love.

Saint Markovia’s Rebellion

Saint Markovia is a legendary figure who led a rebellion against Castle Ravenloft, attempting to strike at Strahd himself. Her history is deeply tied to the village of Krezk and the nearby Abbey, which was originally established to honor her name and light. The Abbott arrived over a century ago with the goal of reopening the Abbey after it had been abandoned. While the Abbey stands as a monument to her, it is now a site of strange laughter, screams, and the Abbott's unorthodox experiments.

The Betrayal of Kavan & The First Folk

Listen well. Long before your kind set foot in this valley, my people — the First Folk — walked these lands. I was chieftain among them, though even in my time the valley was already ancient. We served the Rozana, the Ladies Three: the Seeker, who guides the lost… the Weaver, who breathes life into all things… and the Huntress, who guards the wild places. Our druids tended three sacred fanes hidden across the valley — the Mountain Fane, the Forest Fane, and the Swamp Fane. Each held a gemstone blessed by the Weaver herself, a spark of her power that fed life into the land. This wand… this was carried by those who bound our people to the Rozana. Through it, we spoke to the Ladies — and they answered.

Nearly a thousand years have passed since I rose as chieftain of my tribe. I was young… arrogant… certain that no law of my people could bind me. So I broke them. I walked into the Whispering Wall — that curtain of fog said to hold the corpse of the Devourer, the ancient beast the Ladies slew long before my birth. Inside that mist, I learned secrets no mortal was meant to know. Secrets that showed me how to steal the Huntress’s sacred spear from the Forest Fane… and claim its power for myself. With that power, I waged war on the other tribes. One by one, I bent them beneath my banner.

But the servants of the Seeker — the wereravens — saw what I had become. They hid the life-giving gems of the Fanes so I could not twist their magic to my will. The druids of the First Folk named me traitor. They fled to Soldav, our holiest place in the Balinoks, and there they cast me out. Enemy. Oathbreaker. Unworthy of the graves of my ancestors. When I died, they refused to lay me to rest upon Yester Hill with the great chieftains. Instead, they buried me here — alone, far from my forefathers.

In my final years, another tyrant rose to plague this valley. King Dostron the Hellborn marched in with fire and steel, scattering my people and tearing down their homes. He dammed the Luna River to feed his farmlands, and with every stone he laid, he crushed more of my people’s faith in the Ladies Three. When Dostron finally died, his armies left his fortress to rot, but the First Folk did not recover. Centuries later, when Strahd came with his own armies, many among the druids still carried anger. They sought Strahd’s protection, believing he would restore what had been lost. They were wrong.

A great rift split my people. Those who opposed Strahd retreated to Soldav, our holiest refuge in the Balinok Mountains. Those who embraced him remained in the Svalich Wood. Today, you know them as the Mountain Folk and the Forest Folk. This land… once called Girvisa, ‘the fertile land’… has been twisted by Strahd’s will. He draws power from the land itself, and that bond poisons everything that lives here. If you wish to cleanse that corruption, you must go to Soldav. Only an elder of the Rozana’s faith can teach you what must be done, and only they can summon and hold the mist that hides the village from all outsiders.

Soldav lies somewhere in the Balinoks, veiled by enchanted fog. While the mist stands, no enemy can find it… nor remember its path. I recall only the landmark that marks its entrance: a place where two waterfalls meet as one. But hear me well. Without a token of the Ladies, you will never find the entrance — and the elders of Soldav will never open their gates to you.

The Seance at the Abbey

In the shadowed confines of the Abbey of Saint Markovia, the party sought to peel back the veil of Barovia’s deep mysteries. Guided by the Vistani mystic Zelly, a solemn ritual was performed using chalk-drawn circles and ancient incantations. The atmosphere grew heavy as the presence of a spirit manifested, signaling that the group was no longer alone. What followed was a precise metaphysical inquiry, as the party posed ten pivotal questions to the beyond, each answered by the unfolding of the cards.

  1. Who are you? The cards flipped were the Priest, Missionary, and Shepherd, signifying the presence of Saint Markovia.
  2. Are you Saint Markovia? The Diviner card was flipped, confirming "Yes".
  3. Has the Abbott made a deal with Strahd? The Mist card was drawn, representing something unexpected, mysterious, or unavoidable.
  4. Who is the Abbott? The Healer and Broken One cards were drawn, representing the practice of healing arts but also defeat and the loss of someone important.
  5. What caused the loss of the Abbott? The Marionette and Enchanter cards were drawn, representing the presence of a spy, a fear of failure, or the impact of false information.
  6. How can we restore the Abbott? The Artifact card was drawn, emphasizing a physical object that must be obtained, protected, or destroyed.
  7. Is there something within the Abbey to affect a change to the Abbott? The Anarchist card was drawn, and the answer provided was "No".
  8. Where can we find the artifact? The Dark Lord card appeared, pointing toward the Amber Temple or the dungeons of Ravenloft as the location.
  9. What do we need to do with the artifact? The Warrior and Executioner cards were drawn, indicating that strength, force, or violence may be required, or perhaps the rectification of someone wrongly convicted.
  10. Who needs to be executed/rectified? The cards drawn were the Broken One and the Executioner.

The conclusion of the seance was marked by a rush of ethereal wind and a radiant burst of light that painted the walls in silver and gold. The party was struck by visions: a white-robed woman kneeling before a winged man; a gold-robed woman presenting a silver statuette of a kneeling cleric; the white-robed woman laying bricks upon a wall; and finally, that same woman raising a golden mace toward a shadowed fortress. These images brought surges of humility, hope, defiance, and a sorrow so potent it felt like bitter claws raking at the chest. As the visions faded, the room returned to its former state, leaving the party to grapple with the emotional weight of what they had witnessed.
